# Annual Axes V0.8 — Audit Findings (V0.9 Foundation)

Evidence source: `research/annual-axes/v0.9-foundation/audit/*.json`, generated by
`npm run research:annual-axes-v09:audit-full` over the deterministic 100-chart ×
12-year Nam Phái corpus (`annual-axes-audit-full-v0.8`, seed `20260719`, 1,200
chart-years / 7,200 domain-observations). Regenerated twice with byte-identical
output. All numbers below are read directly from the generated JSON — none are
estimated.

Every finding links to a research question, per the task's §21 requirement.

## Finding 1 — 9 of 28 configured gates fail, all on the same axis

`gate-evaluation.v0.8.json`: 19/28 passed, 9 failed. Every failure is a
**spread/dispersion** gate:

| Gate | Actual | Threshold | Operator |
|---|---:|---:|---|
| meanIntraYearAxisStandardDeviation | 5.90 | 11 | minimum |
| medianIntraYearAxisRange | 15.0 | 30 | minimum |
| p25IntraYearRange | 11.8 | 20 | minimum |
| p10IntraYearRange | 9.0 | 14 | minimum |
| nearDuplicateVectorRate | 0.080 | 0.05 | maximum |
| atLeastTwoOutside42To58Rate | 0.267 | 0.70 | minimum |
| atLeastOneAtOrBelow40Rate | 0.352 | 0.60 | minimum |
| atLeastOneAtOrAbove60Rate | 0.276 | 0.60 | minimum |
| oneLowAndOneHighRate | 0.109 | 0.55 | minimum |

The six domain scores within one chart-year cluster far more tightly around the
neutral midpoint than the gate catalog's own authors expected. `allSixInside45To65Rate`
(24.4%) sits just inside its 25% ceiling — one further point of compression
would fail that gate too.

**Research question:** why is intra-year spread this low? → see Findings 2–3.

## Finding 2 — Annual-layer evidence is net-negative; natal-layer evidence anchors the score and is net-positive

`contribution-mass.v0.8.json` → `perTemporalLayer`:

- `annual` mass ratio (positive/negative weighted) = **0.70** — annual-layer
  stars contribute *more negative* mass than positive.
- `natal` mass ratio = **1.84** — natal stars sitting inside annual-configured
  palaces contribute *far more positive* mass than negative, and this is the
  larger of the two masses (positiveWeightedMass 2,511 vs annual's 3,415 — same
  order of magnitude, but natal is overwhelmingly one-directional while annual
  pulls the other way).

Since natal-layer contribution is by definition **identical every year** for a
given chart (the same static stars sit in the same natal palaces regardless of
`annualYear`), a large, stable, positive natal contribution anchors every
year's score near the same point, while the smaller, negative-leaning annual
layer is the only thing that actually varies year to year — and it isn't large
enough to move the needle past the gate thresholds. This is a direct, testable
root cause for Finding 1.

**Research question:** should natal-layer contribution inside annual palaces be
capped, reweighted, or excluded, so annual-layer signal is not swamped by a
static natal anchor? → `policy/domain-palace-policy.v0.9.json`,
`policy/temporal-identity-policy.v0.9.json`.

## Finding 3 — 12 of 31 catalogued annual stars are "unreachable": Calculation Core emits them in 100% of the corpus, but zero scoring rules reference them

`capability-coverage.v0.8.json`: 13 observed, **13 unreachable**, 5 unsupported.
Of the 13 unreachable, 12 are `supportStatus: "supported"` with
`emissionCount: 1200/1200` (every single chart-year) and
`referencingRuleIds: []`:

Lưu Đào Hoa, Lưu Hồng Loan, Lưu Hỷ Thần, Lưu Kiếp Sát, Lưu Long Đức,
Lưu Nguyệt Đức, Lưu Phúc Đức, Lưu Thiên Đức, Lưu Thiên Hỷ, Lưu Thiên Mã,
Lưu Văn Khúc, Lưu Văn Xương.

(The 13th, **Lưu Thái Tuế**, is correctly unreferenced by any *scoring* rule —
it is deliberately implemented as a prominence multiplier trigger via
`isLuuThaiTueInPalace`, not a matched star registry entry. That is expected
behavior, not a gap.)

This means Calculation Core is *not* the bottleneck for these 12 stars —
production already emits them reliably every year. The V0.8 registry simply
never wired scoring rules for them. This is the most directly actionable,
lowest-risk finding in the whole audit: it requires **zero** new Calculation
Core capability, only new registry rules with a source-backed point class and
polarity.

**Research question:** should these 12 stars receive V0.9 scoring rules? What
point class / polarity / domain should each get? → `policy/star-domain-policy.v0.9.json`.

## Finding 4 — All 70 active production rules are observed at ≥1% frequency; nothing in the live registry is dead weight

`rule-coverage.v0.8.json`: 70/70 rules classified `observed` (0 `rare`,
0 `never-observed`, 0 `unreachable`, 0 `unsupported`). Every rule that is
wired into the registry actually fires often enough to matter across the
deterministic corpus.

**Research question:** none — this is a clean bill of health for the *existing*
registry; it does not need pruning. Contrast with Finding 3, where the gap is
absence, not dead weight.

## Finding 5 — Clamps never trigger; the ±8 raw/palace ceilings are currently decorative

`palaceClampRate = 0`, `axisClampRate = 0` across all 7,200 domain-observations.
The configured `palaceRawClamp` and `axisRawClamp` bands (±8) are never actually
reached by real chart data in this corpus.

**Research question:** are the ±8 clamp bounds miscalibrated (too loose to ever
bind), or is this expected given the current point-class magnitudes? If a V0.9
point-class revision (Finding 3) adds meaningful new positive/negative mass,
should the clamp bounds be revisited so they remain a live safety bound rather
than a no-op? → `policy/thai-tue-policy.v0.9.json` (clamps interact with the
Thái Tuế multiplier), `audit/contribution-mass.v0.8.json`.

## Finding 6 — score === 50 is a mixed population; "scored" state can land on exactly 50 due to floating-point epsilon

`no-signal-analysis.v0.8.json`: of `neutralScoreRate` = 14.27% (1,027 of 7,200
domain-observations score exactly 50), the breakdown is:

- no-signal: 522 (`noSignalRate` 7.25%)
- balanced-signal: 398 (`balancedSignalRate` 5.53%)
- partial-data-at-zero: 0 (Nam Phái never has missing palaces in this corpus —
  see Finding 7)
- **scored: 107** — i.e. `scoreState === "scored"` yet `score === 50`.

Root cause (verified directly): the V0.8 formula compares
`axisRawBeforeThaiTue` / `prominenceAdjustedRaw` to zero with strict
(in)equality, but floating-point summation of weighted contributions can land
on a near-zero epsilon (observed: `5.551115123125783e-17`) instead of exact
`0`. The *score* still correctly rounds to 50.0 (precision washes out the
epsilon), but the *state label* incorrectly reads "scored" for what is really a
balanced-signal case with `matchedStarCount: 3`.

This is **not** a production regression — the public score is unaffected — but
it means `scoreState` is not perfectly reliable as a no-signal/balanced-signal
discriminator at the margin. This is a genuine, source-independent engineering
finding, verified by direct inspection of engine output, not assumed.

**Research question:** should V0.9 tolerance-compare raw values to zero (e.g.
`Math.abs(raw) < 1e-9`) instead of strict equality, to make `scoreState`
epsilon-safe? → candidate category **D. Point policy** in the decision doc.

## Finding 7 — Nam Phái never produces `partial-data` or `unavailable` in this corpus

`unavailableRate = 0`, `partialRate = 0`, `meanResolvedCoverage = 1`,
`partialCoverageDistribution.count = 0` across all 7,200 observations. Every
domain, every chart, every year fully resolves both primary and all
cooperating palaces.

**Research question:** this corpus never exercises the missing-palace paths
(`missing-primary` → `unavailable`, `missing-cooperating` → `partial-data`).
Those paths are covered by existing V0.8 unit/holdout fixtures
(`v08-integrity.test.ts`), not by this distribution corpus — expected, since
the corpus generator always produces well-formed 12-palace charts. Not a gap in
V0.8 itself, just a scope note for this audit's corpus design.

## Finding 8 — Inter-axis correlation is low; shared-palace mapping is not creating redundant axes

`maximumAbsoluteInterAxisCorrelation = 0.325` (family|career), comfortably under
the 0.88 gate ceiling. No pair of domains is behaving as a near-duplicate of
another despite the domain-palace mapping's shared cooperating palaces (e.g.
`Phúc Đức` feeds both `family` and `wealth`).

**Research question:** none — this rules out "shared palace mapping" as an
explanation for Finding 1's low spread; the low spread is a magnitude/mass
problem (Finding 2), not a redundant-axis problem.
