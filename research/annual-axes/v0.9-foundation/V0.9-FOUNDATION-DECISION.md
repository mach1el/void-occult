# Annual Axes V0.9 Foundation — Decision Document

## A. V0.8 audit verdict

- **Full corpus size:** 100 charts × 12 annual years = 1,200 chart-years,
  7,200 domain-observations. Seed `20260719`, tz `+7`, `flowBase: luu-nien`,
  both genders, all 12 hour branches, broad year/month/day spread. Regenerated
  twice with byte-identical output.
- **Gate pass/fail:** 19/28 passed, **9 failed** — all 9 failures are
  intra-year spread/dispersion gates (`meanIntraYearAxisStandardDeviation`,
  `medianIntraYearAxisRange`, `p25IntraYearRange`, `p10IntraYearRange`,
  `nearDuplicateVectorRate`, `atLeastTwoOutside42To58Rate`,
  `atLeastOneAtOrBelow40Rate`, `atLeastOneAtOrAbove60Rate`,
  `oneLowAndOneHighRate`). Every other gate family (boundary rate,
  five-or-more-above-50, all-six-inside-band, inter-axis correlation,
  duplicate-vector, unavailable/partial rate) passes comfortably.
- **Distribution summary:** mean intra-year SD 5.90 (needs ≥11), median range
  15.0 (needs ≥30), max |inter-axis correlation| 0.325 (well under 0.88
  ceiling).
- **No-signal summary:** no-signal 7.25%, balanced-signal 5.53%, neutral-score
  (score===50) 14.27% total — of which 522 are no-signal, 398 balanced-signal,
  **107 are `scoreState: "scored"`** (a verified floating-point epsilon
  artifact, not a doctrinal finding — see Finding 6).
- **Partial/unavailable summary:** both 0% across all 7,200 observations —
  this corpus never exercises the missing-palace paths (those are covered by
  existing V0.8 unit/holdout fixtures instead).
- **Clamp rates:** palace-clamp 0%, axis-clamp 0% — the ±8 clamp bounds never
  trigger anywhere in the corpus.
- **Thái Tuế rate:** 21.18% of domain-observations; never co-occurs with an
  axis clamp.

Full detail: `audit/audit-findings.md` (8 findings, each linked to a research
question), raw JSON in `audit/*.json`.

## B. Root-cause decomposition

| Layer | Verdict |
|---|---|
| **Calculation Core** | Not a bottleneck for 12 of the 17 catalogued annual auxiliary stars — they're emitted in 100% of the corpus. It **is** the bottleneck for the 5 confirmed-unsupported stars (Lưu Đại Hao/Tiểu Hao/Phục Binh/Tuần/Triệt) — zero corpus emissions, no producer exists. |
| **Producer coverage** | 13/31 capability entries `observed`, 13 `unreachable` (12 supported-but-unwired + Thái Tuế, which is correctly excluded from scoring-rule wiring), 5 `unsupported`. |
| **Production registry** | 70/70 active rules `observed` at ≥1% frequency — nothing currently wired is dead weight (Finding 4). The gap is **absence** (12 stars never got a rule), not **decay**. |
| **Domain mapping** | Not implicated — max inter-axis correlation 0.325 shows the shared-palace design (e.g. Phúc Đức feeding both family and wealth) is not producing redundant axes (Finding 8). |
| **Point policy** | Implicated twice: (1) natal-layer contribution is large, positive-leaning, and identical every year, anchoring scores (Finding 2); (2) strict zero-comparison in `scoreState` classification mis-labels ~1.5% of domain-observations under floating-point epsilon (Finding 6). |
| **Temporal identity** | Implicated via Finding 2 — the natal/annual split itself is working as designed, but its consequence (a stable natal anchor + a smaller, net-negative annual delta) is the most likely single explanation for the 9 failed spread gates. |
| **Corpus effects** | The corpus never generates missing-palace conditions (Finding 7) — a scope note, not a defect; Nam Phái's natal-name-based anchor resolution basically guarantees full resolution for any well-formed 12-palace chart. |

## J. Final readiness state

```
RESEARCH_INCOMPLETE
```

**Why not `READY_FOR_V0_9_CANDIDATE`:** the audit half of this task (Parts
A–F) is complete and strongly evidenced, but the doctrine half is not — this
pack has zero verified classical/school-manual sources (`sources/source-review-log.md`),
zero `classical`-status claims, and one real contradiction
(`CONTRA-AAV09-001`) that remains unadjudicated. Recommendation #2 (the
best-evidenced, lowest-Core-risk candidate) cannot be scoped into concrete
per-star rules without source-backed polarity/domain assignments it does not
have.

**Why not `V0_8_SHOULD_REMAIN_UNCHANGED`:** that would undersell real,
well-evidenced findings (Findings 2, 3, 6) that do justify further
investigation — this pack found genuine, measured gaps, not a clean bill of
health across the board.

**Why not `CALCULATION_CORE_BLOCKED` as the overall state:** that verdict
applies precisely and only to the 5 confirmed-unsupported stars (recommendation
#7) — it does not describe the 12-star registry gap (recommendation #2, which
Calculation Core does *not* block) or the point-policy epsilon fix
(recommendation #1, which needs no new Core capability at all).

**Narrow exception:** recommendation #1 (epsilon-tolerant `scoreState`
comparison) needs no new sourcing and is essentially ready as an independent,
small, production-code PR — but it is out of scope for this research-only
task (it touches V0.8 production code) and is called out explicitly in
`prompts/v0.9-candidate-handoff-prompt.md` as a separate, immediately
actionable follow-up.
